            The Harpy Eagle is native to Central and South America, and it is one of the largest living species of eagle.
            It typically inhabits the upper canopy of lowland rainforests, and environment loss has lead to it nearly vanishing from much of Central America.

        

            Ospreys are an order of large, fish-eating raptors. 
            Although they are found on all continents except Antarctica, Ospreys only have four recognized subspecies and are classified as a single species.

        

            Ravens are widely believed to be the best birds of all time, because of how cool they are. [Learn More!]

            

        

            The Loggerhead Shrike is a small predatory bird and it is the only shrike native to North America.
            Because of their small size, they are known for impaling their prey on spikes or thorns to hold it in place while they eat.

        

            The Common Hill-Myna, sometimes called the Hill Myna or Mynah Bird, is a type of Starling native to South and Southeast Asia.
            They are a popular "Talking Bird", being able to mimic many everyday sounds with accuracy and clarity not typically attributed to 
            other talking birds, such as parrots and corvids.
